Arapahoe

Arapahoe Gallery
Designation: Vermach TC-4.

With improved production techniques, advanced movement technology, and a significantly upgraded force-feedback system, the new Arapahoe is quickly being seen as the replacement for the Crusader. While larger than the current version of the Crusader, the Arapahoe is still much smaller than the heavier and more capable Ronin. However, the Arapahoe is a much more versatile chassis than either the Ronin (or it's predecessor, the Kodiak, for that matter) or the current Crusader.

The Arapahoe houses it's primary sensors in the pod located just behind the head. This sensor suite includes the now standard Pan-spectra, Low-light, and IR packages as well as new motion and sonic detection components. The eyepiece in the head of the Arapahoe is mainly used for non-combat operation, as it houses the daylight camera system.
One of the points that makes this chassis so versatile is the number of generic weapon mounts. With two (2) universal mounts on the shins, two (2) on the shoulders, single mounting points on top of the shoulders and a final point in the communications suite, the Arapahoe can be configured for nearly any mission, from long range recon to artillery support.
After the success of the Ronin's CLAW system, a similar weapon was included in the requirements for the 'Corps' newest mecha. Seen here fully deployed, this version is hand held, rather than attached to the forearm. Energy is supplied through the high yield battery pack attached at the bottom. This pack acts as a counterweight as well.
Mobility is always an issue for mecha, and the Arapahoe is no different. However, after the success of the Kodiak split toe design, the feet of the Ronin have not had the same level of performance. Thus, the split toe has reemerged in the Arapahoe's foot design. In addition, increase flexibility in the ankle have allowed for impressive speed gains. Unfortunately, the joints have proved to be relatively fragile requiring increase maintenance and a wary pilot during maneuvers.
Additional changes have been made in the knees of the Arapahoe. While using similar joint technology as that of the Ronin, the armor plate is flexible, allowing greater protection and increased range of motion.
New fuel developments have allowed for the design of a new powerplant. Fueled by two chemicals that,while inert separately, when combined generate a tremendous energy release. These chemicals are kept in fuelcells on either side of the combustion chamber. Heat is still a problem, but nearly to the degree of the Ronin and Kodiak.
Aiding in the dispersion of the Arapahoe's engine heat are three high dissipation vanes, mounted on the rear of the engine pod. The cap of the combustion chamber can be seen in red in this image. The three vanes contain a tremendous amount of surface area due to the nano-fins constructed on the surface of each vane. A slow waving motion along the vanes base moves air across the vanes, causing heat to dissipate more quickly. The constant motion of the vanes has caused them to be dubbed "gills".
Armament for the Arapahoe has been updated. After weaknesses were found in the Browning-Lyfeld M375 pistol of the Ronin, especially to nano-chaff and related visible light jamming systems, a more reliable, less expensive weapon was specified. The result is the Maxton JPG-4. The primary armaments are twin over/under grasers. Tucked underneath the grasers is a small grenade launcher that can fire either high explosive or anti-personel rounds. However the JPG-4 can only be loaded with one or the other. Arapahoes in group exercises vary the loadout from one mecha to another so there is enough of each type of round.
