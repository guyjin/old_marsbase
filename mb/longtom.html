Triton Machine Works' PA-4F (HV & HG) "LongTom"

Initially used as a proof of concept platform for an upcoming mecha, the LongTom is a heavily modified Copperhead. The success of the proof of concept trials prompted the reexamination of the platform. The LongTom was eventually accepted as a new mecha platform completely separate from the Copperhead. The mission is as a mobile firebase to provide frontline support. The LongTom acts as a standoff weapons platform.

The most obvious changes to the LongTom from the Copperhead are the addition of the missle pods. These pods are similar to the single pod carried by the now aging Gekko. The pods of the LongTom are small to some degree, but still carry the 'smartstick' missle, which is now into its second generation. Each pod the LongTom carries can hold up to one hundred twenty-eight (128) individual missles. When fully loaded with pods, the Longtom can carry a total of seven hundred sixty-eight (768) rounds. When combined with the particle cannons that can also be carried, the LongTom can pack a tremendous longrange punch.
The LongTom always carries missle pods, but it can also be fitted with a pair of shoulder mounted particle cannons. This version has been given the HG designation. (The missle only version is the HV type.) These cannons are line-of-sight only and are intended for anti-battery defense. Given that however, crews appear to be very willing to go head to head with heavy armor units. The stopping power of the cannons is formidible. It is important to note that the cannons are actually accellerators that require a short but very measurable amount of time to recharge the capacitors that power it. Additional, multi-stage power systems would improve the recharge time significantly, but would also negatively impact the weight of the LongTom which already is a challenge for air-based transport to carry. Paired with the LongTom's cannons is an air-search sensor suite. This system is able to track incoming projectiles down to the size of a moderately sized bird from an average range of 25 kilometers in flat terrain. The range and accuracy of the sensors give the LongTom a significant ground-to-air threat. While not testing yet under combat conditions, crews are enthusiastic.
Because the LongTom doesn't carry the MagFlux drive units when the cannons are mounted, the need for secondary genertors on the back of the shins is eliminated. In their place are smaller shield generators.
The increased mass of the missle pods, combined with the elimination of the MagFlux have placed a greater strain on the legs and feet of the LongTom. The designs, while a carry over from the Copperhead, have been upgraded to better support the weight. Most noteably, the footpads have been enlarged by a factor of 2x. The new footpads have small AG (Anti-Gravity) lifts in them. These lifts allow for faster movement in the field, an effective answer to the loss of the MagFlux drive.
The removal of the MagFlux drive units hasn't removed the need for a significan power supply. The shoulder mounts for the missle pods are powered. These pods have significant enough mass that the mounts had to be purposly designed to withstand the torque. Similarly, the energy needed to move such masses in controlled manner is significant. By way of comparison, the LongTom's drive unit produces the same output as three (3) Arapahoe class mecha.
